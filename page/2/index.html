Juggling Always Comes in Handy

Today I was at an intern picnic for lunch, celebrating the end of the summer for most interns. I got tired of watching a group of people play Ultimate Frisbee and started juggling some of the Cornhole bags.

A couple of people said “Wow that’s pretty cool”, “How long did that take you?”, “Can you juggle chainsaws/fire/knives?”. Nothing I haven’t heard before. Juggling always brings these questions out from people. Maybe they’ve tried to juggle in the past and gave up (because they probably learned the wrong way) or they haven’t tried before and always wanted to.

Then, out the blue, a fellow intern walks up and asks if I want to pass with him. Like any juggler would, I said sure. When we were passing we talked about where we learned how to juggle, our favorite patterns, etc.

It always suprises me how things that you have done all of your life people can instantly identify with. It really makes the world a smaller place and in some cases helps break the ice.

I’ll be posting more about juggling in the future.

Buy a Megaphone

Going along with my previous post about Estate Sales, one of my best purchases was at one. Buying a heavy-duty, vintage Megaphone. I found it in the basement of someones house just waiting for me to purchase it, and I got it for $30.

At the time it seemed like a sizeable chunk of change to be throwing at something which would certainly be a novelty item or purely for entertainment, but it has come in handy a lot.

There are tons of times in your life where owning a Megaphone can help. Throwing a big party? Need to organize a rally of any sort? Has someone asked you to help them at an organization or event? Chances are you’ll need a megaphone to grab everyones attention. There have been times where people have literally said to me “Man, I wish I had a megaphone for this”, to which I respond that I do, indeed, have one readily available.

Is it used a lot for fun? Sure. My megaphone, in addition to projecting my voice very loudly, comes with some built in sounds (tons of siren noises from the US and UK), and is great for scaring the shit out of people.

All in all, if you ever have the chance to buy one, do it! It will greatly improve your life. Go for broke and get one that runs off of a lantern 6-volt battery and a microphone attached to a coiled cable. Pretty soon you’ll be renting it out to your friends.
