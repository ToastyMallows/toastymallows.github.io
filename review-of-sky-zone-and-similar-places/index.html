Review of Sky Zone (and Similar Places)

For those that don’t know, Sky Zone is a trampoline park that have locations around the US, one recently popped up in Westlake, OH, not far from my house. Some friends and I decided to check it out.

First of all, I would recommend that you go for the $14/hour offer and only get 1 hour to start out with. Extending your stay is easy if you’re having a good time. After an hour (for me at least) you start to get a little tired, and the food there isn’t exactly cheap.

I’m in my early 20s, so jumping around for a little while is pretty easy, but eventually by the end of the hour, my back started to hurt, so keep that in mind.

When you go in, you’ll probably see a lot of young kids, but Sky Zone takes all ages (that will sign or have a waiver signed for them). Trampoline areas are split up by age, so you aren’t jumping around young kids. In addition to just a large trampoline area there is also a smaller dodgeball court, a foam pit and a basketball hoop, all carefully supervised.

Be sure to wear socks because you aren’t wearing your own shoes. Ankle length shoes are provided to all who enter. After a while my ankle was tired of rubbing up against the shoes, as I’m not used to wearing those types of shoes, so maybe put on some longer socks. Also, lockers to hold your loose items (cell phone, wallet, necklace, etc.) aren’t free, but 25 cents is a reasonable price.

To sum up;

Pros:

  • Trampolines
  • Foam pit was awesome
  • Easy waiver process (you can even do it online)
  • Area was very neat (saw people sanitizing things the whole time)
  • Worth the money

Cons:

  • Music, recent pop hits/rap was played
  • Ankle length shoes, though I see the reason behind them (harder to roll your ankle)
  • Tons of rules, but again I see the reason
  • Long wait in some areas (at peak times this could be longer)
  • Lots of young children, again, understandable
  • Trampolines are literally the only thing there, people who aren’t jumping (read: parents) might get bored easily. No TVs, arcade area, etc.

All in all, if you’re looking for a place to jump on a bunch of trampolines, this is definitely the place to go. I think the price is pretty reasonable, but if you’re taking multiple children, this might change. I would go back just for the foam pit.
